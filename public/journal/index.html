Why write it down?

When symptoms go untracked, patterns go unnoticed. Many women wait years before getting an accurate diagnosis — partly because there's no record of what they experienced along the way.

This journal is a first step. Write down what you feel. Note what changed. Keep your own record.

When the time comes, you'll have something to show your doctor — and you'll be the first to know about updates and new resources from Beyond the Hairline.

About this journal

Built for women who
know something is wrong
but can't prove it yet

Scarring alopecia — including CCCA — affects millions of women, disproportionately Black women, and it often goes years without diagnosis. Not because it isn't real, but because patients don't have the documentation to make a case at the doctor's office.

Track It Sis gives you a simple way to track what you're experiencing — your symptoms, their severity, and your notes — so that when you do see a provider, you walk in with something to show.

  • Free to use — always. No account required to journal.
  • Your entries stay on your device. Nothing is sent anywhere without your knowledge.
  • No medical jargon. Plain language, real experience.
  • Leave your email only if you want to hear about future research — and opt out any time.

This is not a medical tool. It does not diagnose, treat, or advise. Please see a board-certified dermatologist specializing in skin of color for any scalp concerns.
